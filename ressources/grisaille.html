A sans serif typeface

This font is an experimentation about thickness, and full and untied parts of a typeface, I tried to exagerate the contrast between thin and thick parts, to accentuate this aspect on the type, but I also made the alternates more linear, to give the choice to the user to see the differance between these contrasts. I also made these alternates to show how much different can be two letters based on the same basis, but made with thickness variation. It is composed of 8 styles (light, regular, medium, bold, and their itali versions). The font is also based, like my previous one, on the roman architecture and egyptian columns, with a strong square base, but with some round esthetics.

Playing with full and untied

The thickness varies between lines and angles, with square based inner shapes. Horizontal, diagonal, and vertical lines / curves are not designed the same thickness. The upper height of the lowercase alphabet is the same than all the uppercase letters. The regular letter is built on a semi round, semi squarish shape, meaning the interior is buit on a square basis, but the exterior is based on a round basis. The alternate letter is built on a fully rounded basis, for the interior and the interior. Some diagonals, like for the «k», «z» and «x» are made thicker than the horizontal lines, to visually arrange the overall letter.

The three dimetion part

I did an experiment using Blender, and used some displaced textures, to create this fur effect, and this bubbly effect. I also used some metallic texture on the letter, and some glass texture do vary the thickness inside the transmissions. The 3d part of the project is meant to represent the digital side of this typeface, but it also considered as a type specimen itself.
